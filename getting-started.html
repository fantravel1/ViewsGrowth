The ViewsGrowth Philosophy

1. Naivety Over Experience

Fresh perspectives outperform industry veterans. When you don't know the "rules," you're free to innovate. The Diary of a CEO team had:

  • No podcasting experience
  • No rulebook to follow
  • Just first principles thinking

Result? They revolutionized podcasting with multi-camera setups, trailers, and visual-first content.

2. Look Outside Your Industry

They studied movies for trailer ideas, not other podcasts. Borrow from industries that have solved similar problems differently.

3. Experimentation Over Perfection

Launch with 80% quality and improve every week. Perfect videos that never ship earn zero views.

Channel Setup Checklist

Week 1: Foundation

  • Choose niche with 10K+ monthly searches
  • Research 10 successful channels in your space
  • Create channel name (memorable, searchable)
  • Design basic banner and profile pic
  • Write channel description with keywords
  • Set up channel trailer spot
  • Create About page with contact info
  • Install basic analytics

Week 2: Content Planning

  • Generate 20 video ideas minimum
  • Research keywords for each idea
  • Create content calendar (2 videos/week)
  • Design thumbnail templates
  • Write first 4 video scripts
  • Plan your signature style elements

Week 3-4: Production Sprint

  • Film first 4 videos in batch
  • Edit with consistent style
  • Create compelling thumbnails
  • Write SEO-optimized descriptions
  • Schedule uploads at optimal times
  • Prepare social media promotion

Your First 30 Days: Momentum Builder

Days 1-7: Setup & Research

  • Complete channel setup
  • Study 50 videos in your niche
  • Note patterns in titles, thumbnails, hooks
  • Join creator communities

Days 8-14: Create Your First Videos

  • Film 4 videos (batch day)
  • Focus on clear audio and good lighting
  • Keep videos 5-8 minutes initially
  • Perfect your intro hook (5 seconds)

Days 15-21: Launch & Learn

  • Publish first 2 videos
  • Share in relevant communities
  • Respond to every comment
  • Analyze retention graphs
  • Note drop-off points

Days 22-30: Optimize & Scale

  • Implement feedback in new videos
  • Test different thumbnail styles
  • Experiment with titles
  • Plan next month's content
  • Set up basic automation

10 Beginner Mistakes That Kill Channels

  1. Obsessing Over Equipment
    Content quality beats production quality every time. Start with what you have.
  2. Irregular Upload Schedule
    Consistency builds trust with viewers and the algorithm. Pick a schedule and stick to it.
  3. Ignoring Analytics
    Your retention graph tells you exactly what works. Study it after every video.
  4. Copying Successful Creators
    Learn from them, but develop your unique voice. Authenticity wins long-term.
  5. Weak Thumbnails
    You could have the best content ever, but bad packaging = no clicks.
  6. No Clear Niche
    The algorithm doesn't know who to show random content to. Pick a lane initially.
  7. Giving Up Too Early
    Remember the hockey stick. Most quit right before the growth curve.
  8. Not Engaging With Comments
    Early community building is crucial. Reply to everyone in the first 2 hours.
  9. Chasing Viral Videos
    Consistency beats virality. Build for loyal viewers, not one-time viewers.
  10. Perfectionism Paralysis
    Your 50th video will be 10x better than your 1st. You need those 49 to learn.

5 Quick Wins for New Channels

1. The 5-Second Hook

Start with your most interesting point. No long intros, no channel updates. Jump straight into value.

2. Pattern Interrupts

Change visuals every 15-20 seconds. Use B-roll, graphics, or camera angle changes to maintain attention.

3. End Screen Strategy

Last 20 seconds should push to another video. Increases session time = algorithm love.

4. Community Tab

Available at 500 subscribers. Use it for polls, updates, behind-the-scenes to build connection.

5. Shorts as Discovery

Create Shorts from your best moments. Different audience pool, same monetization potential.
